K7UAZ Candidate Radar Feature Review

The review packet starts with known telemetry and then compares candidate radar features against expected balloon position, altitude, and timing. The strongest review item is the cross-radar association A001: KEMX_T004 + KIWA_T001.

Horizontal position comes from Maidenhead grid centers, not exact GPS. Telemetry speed and derived grid-center speed should be treated as approximate, especially across the 90-minute reporting gap.

Evidence Audit v1.1

An independent second-opinion audit that scores each candidate purely on extracted radar evidence — not on the detector's own ranking — to catch circular reasoning. Each candidate gets an evidence_score from 7 positive criteria and 6 false-positive flag penalties.

New ✓

How the independent score works

Each candidate earns +1 point for every positive radar criterion (good altitude agreement, plausible reflectivity, cross-radar support, etc.) and loses 1 point for every triggered false-positive flag (weather contamination, ground clutter, biological echo, anomalous propagation, isolated artifact, impossible jump).

The final verdict_v1_1 is compared against the v1 verdict that was based on the detector's own review_priority_score, and any upgrade or downgrade is highlighted with a badge.

A001 — the detector's top-ranked candidate — was downgraded in v1.1 due to ground clutter, biological echo risk, and an impossible trajectory jump flag despite strong altitude and offset scores.
